Entropy

Entropy is a thermodynamic quantity representing the unavailability of a system's thermal energy for conversion into mechanical work, often interpreted as the degree of disorder or randomness in the system. It is lack of order or predictability; gradual decline into disorder.

Phase Changes

Increaing Entropy

Solid --> Liquid --> Aqueous --> Gas

Increase in temperature or pressure results in a increase in entropy.

Equilibrium

Equilibrium is the point at which the concentrations of reactants and products do not change with time. It appears as though the reaction has stopped but in fact the rates of the forward and reverse reactions are equal so reactants and products are being created at the same rate.

Heat of Reaction

The difference between the potential energy of the products and the potential energy of reactants.

The higher the amount of heat the more energy a substance contians.

Increasing Energy

Solid --> Liquid --> Aqueous --> Gas

Endothermic and Exothermic Reaction

Endothermic

The absorbtion of heat within a reaction.

Exothermic

The release of heat within a reaction.

Reactions

In order for a reaction to occur, there must always be an effective collision between reacting particles.

Reaction rate increases as the number of effective collisions increases.

An increase in pressure, temperature or surface area will increase the reaction rate.

If the concentration of one of the reactants in a chemical reaction is increased, the rate of the reaction will increase.

The use of a catalyst will speed up the reaction reducing the activation energy.
