Acids, Bases , and Salts

Acids will always have/donate a hydrogen ion (H+) to a hydroxide ion (OH-).

Acids are Proton Donors.

The more acidic a solution the lower the pH level.

Bases will always have a hydroxide ion (OH-) and recieve a hydrogen ion (H+).

Bases are Proton Acceptors.

The more basic a solution the higher the pH level.

Neutral substances have a pH of 7.

Electrolytes

A chemical compound that conducts electricity by changing into ions when melted or dissolved into a solution.

Electrolytes are made up of common bases or common acids

Strong Electrolytes are electrolytes that are heavily basic or heavily acidic with pH levels much higher or lower and 7.

Stronger Electrolytes are more conductable.

Weak Electrolytes are electrolytes that are more neutral with pH levels much closer to 7.

Weaker Electrolytes are less conductable.

Neutralization Reaction

Acid + Base --> Salt + Water

Make sure the equation is balanced and both sides of the reaction are equal.

Molar Ratio should be 1:1 for the reaction to nuetralize.

In a solution the concentration of hydrogen ions (H+) and hydroxide ions (OH-) should have a sum of 14.

Forward Reaction is from left to right.

Reverse Reaction is from right to left.
